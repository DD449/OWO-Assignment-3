Project Description

‘Sports Hub’ is an application that assists people in finding various public sporting areas. The type of areas it will include will be, football ovals, soccer pitches, tennis courts and basketball courts and the App will be able to give you directions/information to each public sporting area. This is to promote the use of public sporting areas and get people to socialise through common interests. It does this by helping a user find Clubs and/or Coaches for their desired sports.

Joining a Club will allow you to be part of an official club that participates regularly and participant in entry level programs for each sport. You can find a club by a browse function where you search for it using a search bar that matches the words to appropriate teams or if a user is unsure but really wants to participate in a sport they can use a fun quiz inside the app in order to be matched to a club and a sport.

A user will be able to book appointments with coaches and book Indoor areas for their sport. When a user books a coach they can book them as a General inquiry where it would be similar to an introductory lesson where the user would get the general idea how and what will happen in their coaching sessions. They can book as a Personal training session where a user will be able to train 1-on-1 on fundamental skills of their chosen sports and Group training sessions where the user can train as part of a group and further enhance their Fundamentals and Team skills required for the sport.

A user can also create teams and join teams for various sports at a Beginner, Intermediate and Advanced level. The App also maintains a leaderboard similar to the likes of Uber where a user can give other users a star rating so people can spend the limited time they may have for a sport optimally. A user can use in-app messaging to talk to and maintain contacts that they have met through the app. Ultimately, participating in a sport ‘Sports Hub’ will make it easier to Enter and easier to gain more knowledge about any chosen sports that are featured on the App.

Topic

‘Sports Hub’ is designed for sports lovers, As in Australia more than 90% of adults have an interest in sports (Australian Government, Department of Health) but it seems hard to begin getting into sports as you get older. This app makes this process much easier.

Community sport infrastructure is estimated to generate an annual value of more than 16.2 billion to Australia. With 6.3 billion worth of economic benefit (Australian Government: Australian Sports Commision, The Value of Community Sport Infrastructure, 2018) Making this app tap into a billion dollar market.

Motivation

‘Sports Hub’ gives people an outlet to be more active. In 2017–18, an estimated 2 in 3 (67%) Australians aged 18 and over were overweight or obese (36% were overweight but not obese, and 31% were obese). That’s around 12.5 million adults. (Australian Government: Australian Institute of Health and Welfare, Overweight and obesity, 2020).

Almost 4 in 10 adults aged 18–64 (37%) reported Not enough time or Too many other commitments as the main barriers to participating in sport or recreational physical activities. As age increased, Poor health or injury was more frequently cited as the main barrier—increasing from almost one-fifth (18%) of those aged 35–44 to almost half (48%) of adults aged 65 and over. (Australian Government: Australian Institute of Health and Welfare, Physical activity across the life stages, pg.7). This app will make it easier to have enough time because it will be easier to track where to start from and reduce poor health or injury barrier by having an overall more healthy population as the App makes it easier to be active.

This app uses models demonstrated by Uber and similar apps but pigeon-holes it to physical activity in particular sports and participating in them. A future employer will see this as an innovation combining coaching and team finding in the one App tapping into a profitable market. Ultimately showing that we are forward thinkers.

(Australian Government: Australian Institute of Health and Welfare, Overweight and obesity, 2020)
https://www.aihw.gov.au/reports/australias-health/overweight-and-obesity

LandScape

This app is similar to Uber in terms of how it works with map integration and reviewing purposes in order to help people complete the task of finding a sports team, club or coach optimally. In the market there’s an App called Teamer which finds Teams and an App called ‘Possible’ that finds Personal trainers. We can use these as guidelines to create our App that is unique to them both by hitting both markets and being an overall more accessible and encompassing version for users to interact with.
